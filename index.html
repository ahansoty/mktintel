Executive Summary

Sector-wide performance snapshot · Q1 2026

CRE Investment Q4 2025 $171.6B ▲ 29% QoQ — CBRE
Office CMBS Delinquency 12.34% All-Time High — Trepp Jan 2026
Industrial Net Absorption 176.8M SF ▲ 16.3% YoY — Cushman & Wakefield

The U.S. commercial real estate market enters Q1 2026 at a critical inflection point. After years of post-pandemic recalibration, multiple sectors show simultaneous recovery signals — yet deep structural challenges persist, particularly in office CMBS. CRE investment surged 29% in Q4 2025 to $171.6 billion (CBRE), signaling improving confidence across the capital stack.

The office sector is showing its clearest recovery signals since 2020, while retail remains near historic vacancy lows. Industrial is entering a new expansion cycle after a supply correction. Hotels face a modest RevPAR recovery in 2026 after the first decline since 2020. CMBS delinquencies are expected to peak in 2026 before normalizing.

Q4 2025 Summary (Source: Cushman & Wakefield): LA's office market closed out 2025 with less momentum than anticipated. A year-end increase in vacant inventory returning to the market resulted in over 1.0 million square feet of negative absorption in Q4 2025. This pushed overall vacancy to 23.4% — a 50-basis-point increase quarter-over-quarter, though still 10 bps below year-end 2024.

The steady decline of new sublease inventory observed throughout most of 2025 reversed modestly at year-end. No new office space was delivered in Q4 2025, and none over the course of the year. The near-term development pipeline remains limited, with the most notable project being 1950 Avenue of the Stars in Century City, expected to deliver 1.6 MSF of new inventory to the LA West market in 2026.

Upcoming completions are concentrated in prime submarkets where pre-leasing levels are already meaningful, limiting the near-term supply risk from new construction.

El Segundo Submarket Commentary (Q4 2025 · Source: Cushman & Wakefield): As of Q4 2025, the El Segundo market had a total office inventory of 11.5 million square feet. The vacancy rate stands at 26.8% — significantly above the broader LA average of 23.4%. Q4 net absorption came in at negative 53,000 SF, continuing a pattern of tenant contraction. YTD 2025 net absorption was negative 322,000 SF, indicating meaningful tenant downsizing activity throughout the year.

The overall Class A asking rental rate is $4.09/SF/month FSG. Despite soft market fundamentals, El Segundo continues to attract defense, aerospace, and technology tenants — many renewing significant blocks of space (Northrop Grumman: 124,400 SF renewal at 3701 Doolittle Dr in Q4 2025).

PCT Property Context: The subject property at 100, 200 & 222 North PCH Highway (1.607 MSF total) is a significant presence in this submarket. PCT achieved a NOI of $16.65M in 2025 — up 22.2% versus 2024's $13.6M — as leasing momentum improved mid-year. Occupancy across the three buildings averaged approximately 48.6% as of December 2025.

Executive Summary

Sector-wide performance snapshot · Q1 2026

CRE Investment Q4 2025 $171.6B ▲ 29% QoQ — CBRE
Office CMBS Delinquency 12.34% All-Time High — Trepp Jan 2026
Industrial Net Absorption 176.8M SF ▲ 16.3% YoY — Cushman & Wakefield

The U.S. commercial real estate market enters Q1 2026 at a critical inflection point. After years of post-pandemic recalibration, multiple sectors show simultaneous recovery signals — yet deep structural challenges persist, particularly in office CMBS. CRE investment surged 29% in Q4 2025 to $171.6 billion (CBRE), signaling improving confidence across the capital stack.

The office sector is showing its clearest recovery signals since 2020, while retail remains near historic vacancy lows. Industrial is entering a new expansion cycle after a supply correction. Hotels face a modest RevPAR recovery in 2026 after the first decline since 2020. CMBS delinquencies are expected to peak in 2026 before normalizing.
